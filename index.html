Technologies

Knowledge Graph

OpenDrugAI builds a truly huge biomedical knowledge graph with integrated data for drug discovery and development process, and it continually becomes smarter and faster. Intuitively, any two nodes in a knowlege graph may be linked in a path like Compound A binds Protein B, Protein B interacts with Protein C, Protein C paticipate in Pathway D, then AD have a relationship. Those pathes will help to get knowledge directly like using search engine. Algorithmically, in a large scale, AI based on graph neural networks is employed to reveal novel interaction paterns, which will revolutionize the intractable problems facing drug discovery an development area, such as drug repurposing and bioactive compound target prediction.

Molecular Representation & Design

Leveraging cutting-edge AI technologies, specifically reinforcement learning, graph attention networks and GAN, molecular representations are capable of gaining much more insights from molecular structure than ever, and novel structures with desired drug-likeness and synthetic accessibility properties can be designed and generated for targeted bioactivity test. These technologies are transforming virtual screening, ADME/T prediction and lead optimization.

Targeting PPIs

The successes of therapeutic antibodies suggest PPIs (protein-protein interactions) are promising targets especially for cell surface receptors. As per protein-protein "interactomes", the number of putative PPIs are predicted in the range of 130,000 through 650,000. In front of us is a sea of druggable targets, but it is still challenging to exploit PPI interfaces for effective PPI modulators. AI technologies, such as graph convolutional networks can predict protein binding energy at an unprecedented accuraccy, which is key to hotspots identification, laying the foundation for futher design of PPI-targeted small molecules or conformationally-constrained peptides.

Lab Automation

Labs in China are running out of cheap student labor, we are seeking to optimize experimental pipelines for automation, so as to reduce labor cost and improve efficiency. Open-hardware movement provides low-cost lab kits for DIY and by introducing AI, those low-cost lab kits can be as trustworthy as manufactured machines. Most importantly, those equipments that aided scientists won Nobel Prizes are not manufactured, but invented, so why not DIY with the help of AI?

Drug Innovation Index

We build an index that quantifies the gaps between unmet medical needs and current drug development markets, which could hugely help decide where the money should go, for picking the low-hangging fruit or for shooting stars. We developed a novel algorithm to hint the unmet medical needs not like previous conventional methods that see most prescribed drugs.

Our Story

OpenDrugAI is a Shanghai-based not-for-profit think tank that researches AI-based solutions for drug discovery and development. Since professionals owning expertise in deep learning, massive biomedical data mining and hardware prototyping for real problems in drug discovery and development are extremely scarce. We strive to create a climate for interdisciplinary innovations by gathering more smart people to tackle those pressing problems.
